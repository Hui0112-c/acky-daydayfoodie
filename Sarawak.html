Kolo Mee

Kolo Mee is a famous Sarawak noodle dish, especially popular in Kuching. It features springy egg noodles tossed in light sauce and oil. It usually topped with minced pork, char siu (barbecued pork) and green onions. The dish is loved for its fragrant aroma, chewy texture and savory-sweet taste.

Kampua Mee

Kampua Mee is a traditional noodle dish from Sarawak, especially popular in Sibu. It features springy noodles tossed in lard or oil, soy sauce and shallot oil, then topped with slices of pork and green onions. The dish is loved for its simplicity, fragrant aroma and comforting taste.

Prawn Tomyam Noodle

Prawn Tomyam Noodle is a spicy-sour noodle dish inspired by Thai cuisine and popular in Malaysia. It features noodles served in a tangy broth made from lemongrass, lime, chili, herbs and prawns as the main highlight. The soup is loved for its bold, refreshing flavors that combine sourness, spiciness and savory richness.

Tomato Kueh Tiaw

Tomato Kueh Tiaw is a noodle dish popular in Sarawak, especially in Kuching. Unlike the Penang-style Kueh Tiaw, this version is made with flat rice noodles stir-fried in a sweet and tangy tomato-based sauce. It is usually served with chicken, seafood, and vegetables, making it both flavorful and comforting.

Layered Cake

Image source: F&Life Website


Layered Cake also known as Kek Lapis. It is made by baking thin layers of cake stacked together. Unlike the Indonesian version, Sarawak's Kek Lapis is famous for its bright colors and creative patterns. It often flavored with pandan, chocolate or vanilla to make it both delicious and visually stunning.

Bubur Kuali

Image source: Jordannn's post on Facebook


Bubur Kuali is a traditional dessert made by slow-cooking rice flour and coconut milk in a large wok. It has a thick, creamy texture and is sweetened with palm sugar. The dessert is loved for its rich, fragrant and comforting flavor.
