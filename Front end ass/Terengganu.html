Main Dishes

Image source: Munch Malaysia Website

Laksam Terengganu

Laksam Terengganu is a noodle dish that made with thick and flat rice flour rolls. It is served with a rich and creamy white gravy made from ground fish and coconut milk. The dish is usually topped with fresh herbs and eaten with sambal for extra flavor.

Satar

Satar is a traditional dish made from a mixture of fish, grated coconut, chili and spices. The mixture is wrapped in banana leaves and grilled over charcoal. It is loved for its smoky aroma, spicy-sweet taste and tender texture.

Image source: Discovery Terengganu Website

Nasi Dagang

In Terengganu, Nasi Dagang is made with white fragrant rice mixed with glutinous rice, steamed with coconut milk and fenugreek seeds. It is usually served with gulai ikan tongkol (tuna curry) and pickled vegetables (acar).

Desserts & Snacks

Lompat Tikam Terengganu

Image source: kami anak TERENGGANU blog


Lompat Tikam is a traditional Terengganu dessert made with layers of green rice flour jelly, white coconut milk layer and red glutinous rice. It is sweet, creamy, and colorful. It often enjoyed chilled as a refreshing treat.

üçΩÔ∏è Kedai Makan Wan Sabil

Akok Terengganu

Image source: Jengok Dapur's post on Facebook


Akok Terengganu is made of eggs, coconut milk, flour and sugar, but it uses less palm sugar compared to the Akok Kelantan. This makes it lighter in color and less sweet. It has a soft and fluffy texture with a mild and fragrant taste.

Kuih Nekbat

Image source: ikanmas.in Website


Kuih Nekbat is a traditional Terengganu dessert made from rice flour, eggs and sugar. The batter is baked into small golden cakes, then soaked in thick sugar syrup to give it a soft, sweet and slightly chewy texture. It is usually enjoyed during festive seasons or special occasions.

Pulut Lepa

Image source: Lemon8 Website


Pulut Lepa is a Terengganu snack made of glutinous rice with spiced fish filling. It always wrapped in banana leaves and grilled. Though usually eaten as a snack, it can also be filling enough to enjoy as a light meal.

Karipap Ikan

Image source: WordPress Website


Karipap Ikan is a savory pastry from Terengganu filled with spiced fish, usually tuna or mackerel. The fish filling is wrapped in a flaky pastry and deep-fried until golden and crispy. It is loved for its crunchy exterior with flavorful and mildly spicy fish filling.
