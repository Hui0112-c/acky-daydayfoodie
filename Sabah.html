Ngiu Chap

Ngiu Chap is a traditional Sabahan beef noodle soup that literally means "mixed beef". It is made with various cuts of beef and beef innards, simmered in a rich, flavorful broth. The dish is usually served with noodles and garnished with vegetables and herbs, making it hearty and comforting.

Tuaran Mee

Tuaran Mee is a well-known noodle dish from Tuaran, Sabah. The freshly made noodles are eggy and springy, stir-fried until slightly crispy for extra texture. It is commonly served with pork, egg and vegetables to create a rich and savory flavor.

Hinava Sabah

Hinava is a traditional Sabahan dish made from raw fish, mackerel, marinated in lime juice, ginger, chili and shallots. The acidity of the lime "cooks" the fish to give it a fresh and tangy flavor. It is a signature dish of the Kadazan-Dusun people and is often enjoyed during festive occasions.

Linopot

Linopot is a traditional Sabahan dish of the Kadazan-Dusun people. It is rice, sometimes mixed with sweet potato or yam, wrapped neatly in leaves such as tarap or irik leaves. This dish is unique not only for its fragrant leaf wrapping but also because it was originally made as a convenient, portable meal for farmers working in the fields.

Kuih Cincin

Kuih Cincin is a traditional Sabahan snack shaped like small rings. It is made from rice flour, brown sugar and coconut milk, then deep-fried until crispy. The snack is loved for its crunchy texture and sweet, caramel-like flavor.

Durian Udang Merah

Image source: MedikGo Website


Durian Udang Merah is a unique durian variety from Sarawak, known for its bright reddish-orange flesh. It has a rich, creamy texture with a strong aroma and bittersweet taste. This rare fruit is highly prized by durian lovers for its distinctive flavor and vibrant color.
